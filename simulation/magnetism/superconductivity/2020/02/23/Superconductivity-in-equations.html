Superconductivity

Properties (bulk)
Perfect conductivity of lelectricity below $T_c, H_c, J_c$ (temperature,field,current density)
Fully expels magnetic field ($B=0$ inside).

Methods to verify properties
Infrared absorption = Energy gap $\Delta$ measurement (since 1950s)
Ultrasound absorption (phonon absorption) = Gives $T_c$ Tunneling (NS interface) = $N(E)$ Density of states
Angular resolved Photon emission spectroscopy = $k$-dependence/orientation dependence of $\Delta$
NMR = Coherence length $\xi$- measurement
XRD = Crystal structure measurement (does not change on transition)
STM = Abrokosov lattice (hexagonal) measurement in type II SCs. Electron tunneling = The gap measurement

Type I and Type II

Type I:
Pure metals
Perfect diamagnets ($\chi_{SC} = -1$)
$\lambda < \frac{\xi}{\sqrt{2}}$\

Type II:
Alloys/intermetallic
Two critical fields (SC state, vortex state,normal state)
$\lambda > \frac{\xi}{\sqrt{2}}$

Superconductor + Maxwell + 2-fluid model = London equations

Only normalconducting electrons:

\[\sigma \rightarrow \infty\] \[j \rightarrow finite\] \[E \rightarrow 0\] \[B \rightarrow constant\]

Cannot be explained (but is consistent with) classical electrodynamics.

Maxwell:

\[\nabla\times \mathbf{B} = \mu_0 \left(\mathbf{J}_s + \ptpt{\mathbf{D}}{t}\right)\] \[\nabla\times \mathbf{E} = -\ptpt{\mathbf{B}}{t}\] \[\nabla\cdot \mathbf{B} = 0\]

And assumptions (two fluid model: separate superconducting electrons and very small displacement current/slowly changing mag field and low $vB$):

\[\ptpt{\mathbf{D}}{t}\rightarrow 0 \Leftrightarrow \ptpt{^2 \mathbf{B}}{t^2}\rightarrow 0\] \[\mathbf{J}_s = n_s e^* \mathbf{v}_s\] \[m^*\dot{\mathbf{v}}_s = e^* E\]

where $e^* $, $m^* $ are the effective charge, mass. We get for the $E$, $B$-fields (and the London equations for $J_s$)

\[\mathbf{B} = \frac{m}{\mu_0 n_s e^2}\nabla^2 \mathbf{B}; \qquad \mathbf{E} = \frac{m}{n_se^2} \mathbf{\dot{J}_s};\qquad \nabla\times\mathbf{J_s} = -\mu_0\lambda_L^2\mathbf{B}\]

in a free-charge less material ($\rho_f=0$):

\[\nabla^2\mathbf{E} = \lamba_L^2 \mathbf{E}\]

For a 1D wall, we get (the mag field $B=0$ deep inside the bulk superconductor):

\[B(x) = B_{ext} e^{-x/\lambda_L}\]

where $\lambda_L = \sqrt{\frac{m}{\mu_0 n_s e^2}}$.

assume that at $T=0$, $n_s = n_{tot}$: \(\lambda_L(0) = \sqrt{\frac{m}{\mu_0 n e^2}};\qquad\)

Two-fluid model

Assume Drude model for 2 fluids (normal electrons $n_n$, superconducting electrons $n_s$):

\[m \frac{d\mathbf{v}_i}{dt} = eE - m\frac{\mathbf{v}_i}{\tau_i}\]

with \(\mathbf{J}_i = n_i e \mathbf{v}_i\) and $\tau_s\rightarrow \infty$ and fourier transforming keeping directions intacts and \(\mathbf{J}_i(\omega) = \sigma_i \mathbf{E}(\omega)\) where $\sigma_i = \sigma_{1i} - i \sigma_{2i}$

\[\sigma_{1s} = \frac{\pi}{2} \frac{n_s e^2}{m}\delta(\omega);\qquad \sigma_{2s} = \frac{n_s e^2}{m\omega}\]

Assuming $\omega \ll \frac{1}{\tau_n}$ (normally $\tau_n \approx 10^{-19}$

\[\sigma_{1n} = \frac{\tau_n e^2 n_n}{m}; \sigma_{2n} = 0\]

Temperature dependencies are (in Gorter-Casimir model) just below $T_C$ $n_n = t^4$ and $n_s = (1-t^4)$ where $t=\frac{T}{T_c}$

Power dissipated per unit volume is \(\rho \lvert J\rvert^2= Re(1/\sigma^2)\lvert J\rvert^2\) so that total power loss is \(P_{loss} \approx \sigma_N\frac{\mu_0^2 \omega^2}{\lambda_L^4} \lvert J\rvert^2 V\) where $V$ is the volume.

Thermodynamical work of magnetic field + state energy difference

Thermodynamics with Gibbs free energy per volume $g$ and $M = \chi H_{ext}$ and $B=\mu_0(H_{ext} + M)$. Work done by placing material in field

\[W = -\mu_0 \int_0^{H_{ext}} MdH = -\mu_0 \chi \frac{H_{ext}^2}{2}\]

Using $g_s(T,H_{ext}) = g_s(T,0) + W_{H_{ext}}$ and the $\chi\ll 1$ for the normal state and $H_c := H_{ext}$ when $g_s(T,H_{ext})=g_n(T,H_{ext})$

\[g_n(T,0)-g_s(T,0) = \frac{\mu_0(1+\chi_n) H_c^2}{2}\]

which is really really small.

For a thin-film ($d\ll \lambda$) you can find (because Magnetization is way lower and $B$ does not tend to $0$ inside):

\[H_{c,\parallel} = \sqrt{24}H_c\lambda/d\]

Critical field at temperature

\[H_c(T) = H_c(T=0)\left[1-\left(\frac{T}{T_c}\right)^2\right]\]

Thermodynamical specific heat (+measurement of discontinuity)

Volume constant during change of internal energy/adding heat:

\[c_V = \frac{dQ}{MdT} = \frac{dU}{dT}\]

Non-local electrodynamics

\[\sigma_p \sim \frac{\sigma_E}{v_F} \sim \frac{k_B T_c}{v_F}\] \[\sigma_x \sim \xi = \mathcal{O}(1)\cdot \frac{\hbar v_F}{k_B T_c}\] \[\mathbf{J}_s(\mathbf{r}) = C \int \frac{\mathbf{r}(\mathbf{r}\cdot \mathbf{A}(\mathbf{r}'))}{r^4} \mathrm{e}^{r/\xi} d^3 \mathbf{r}'\]

instead of

\[\mathbf{J}_s(\mathbf{r}) = \sigma E(\mathbf{r})\]

Evidence for phonon interaction

\[\Delta T_c/T_c = -\frac{1}{2} \frac{\Delta M}{M}\]

$M$ is the mass of an ion, $\Delta M$ is the difference in ion mass for a different isotope.

Cooper instability

Screened Coloumb potential (always positive = always repulsive):

\[V_C=\frac{V_{\mathbf{q}}}{\mathcal{E}(\mathbf{q},\omega)}\]

Electron phonon interaction (can be negative when $\omega^2<\omega_D^2$:

\[V_{e-ph} = \frac{2\omega_D M_q^2}{\mathcal{E}(\mathbf{q},\omega)(\omega^2- \omega_D^2(\mathbf{q}))}\]

Scattering of the minimum 2-electron wavefunction/particles (assuming that symmetric wavefunction = lowest energy and up-down spin is only one that can bind)
and the interaction is spin-independent and $\ket{i}, \ket{f}$ are plane wave states:

\[\left(H_{e-e}^{direct}\right)_{if} = \bra{i} H_{e-e}^{direct} \ket{f} = \int e^{i\mathbf{k}\cdot \mathbf{r}} U_{Coloumb}(\mathbf{r})e^{-i\mathbf{k}'\cdot \mathbf{r}}d^3r\]

Scattering of 2 electrons through indirect process:

\[\frac{\bra{i}H\ket{int}\bra{int}H\ket{f}}{(E_i-E_{int})^2}\]

The singlet wavefunction

\(\ket{\Psi_0} = \sum_{k>k_F} g_k c^\dagger_{k\uparrow}c^\dagger_{-k\downarrow} \ket{0}\) where $\ket{0}$ is the filled Fermi Sea, $\ket{\Psi_0}$ is the superconducting many body ground state, $g_k$ a (filling) constant and $c^\dagger_{k\uparrow}c^\dagger_{-k\downarrow}$ the creation operators of a $(k\uparrow,-k\downarrow)$ pair.

For $N$ electrons, you can make $N/2$ different pairs.

\[\ket{\Psi_G} = \prod_{k_1,k_2,\ldots,k_N} (u_{k_i}+v_{k_i}c^\dagger_{k_i\uparrow})c^\dagger_{k_i\downarrow}\ket{0}\]

Determining $T_c$

From BCS somewhere:

\[E_k = \sqrt{\xi_k^2 + \Delta^2(T)}; \qquad \Delta(T\rightarrow T_c)=0\]

where does the below equation comes from?

\[\frac{1}{V} = \frac{1}{2}\sum_k \frac{\tanh(\beta E_k/2)}{E_k} = N(E_F)\int_0^{\beta_c \hbar \omega_D/2} \frac{\tanh(x)}{x}dx\]

from this

\[k_B T_c = 1.13 \hbar \omega_D e^{-1/(N(E_F)V)}\]

Also from something different $\Delta(T=0) = 2\hbar \omega_D e^{-1/(N(E_F)V)}$ for $NV\ll 1$:

\[\Delta(0) = 1.764k_B T_c\]

Close to $T=0$

\[\Delta(T) = 1.74\Delta_0\sqrt{1-\frac{T}{T_c}}\]

Determining specific heat $c_v$

Assume $E_k = \sqrt{\xi_k^2+\Delta^2(T)}$ and a F-D distribution for the qps $f_k :=f(E_k) = \frac{1}{1+\exp(\beta E_k)}$ and entropy being $S=-k_b \sum_i p_i \ln(p_i) $ and specific heat $C_v = TdS/dT = -\beta dS/d\beta$. Electronic contribution to entropy in superconducting state (every qp in up/down spin occupied or not):

\[S_{e,sc} =-2k_b \sum_k (1-f_k)\ln(1-f_k) + f_k \ln(f_k)\]

Note that $\frac{dE_k}{d\beta}\neq 0$ and that chain rules can be applied a lot and $\xi_k$ indep of $\beta$:

\[c_v = -2\beta k_b \sum_k E_k \ptpt{f_k}{E_k} (E_k + \frac{\beta}{2E_k}\ptpt{\Delta^2(T)}{\beta})\]

Now we need to use the sum_k to int over energies and find for T«T_c, T just below T_c, T above T_c (TBD):

\(C(T\llT_c) = \gamma T = \frac{2\pi^2}{3}N(\xi_k)k_b^2T\)$

\[C(T\uparrow T_c) = N(\xi_F)k_B \beta^2 \frac{d\Delta^2}{d\beta}\int_{-\infty}^\infty \ptpt{f}{\chi}d\chi\] \[\frac{\Delta c}{C_{e,norm}} = \frac{9.4}{2\pi/3}\]

The quantum model (aka Landau-Ginzburg theory)

This theory is only completely valid at $T\approx T_c$

The ground state wave function ($n_s$ constant over space):

\[\psi_s(x,t) = |\psi|e^{i\phi}; \qquad |\psi(x,t)|^2 = n_s(x,t) \qquad \nabla \psi_s = i|\psi|e^{i\phi}\nabla \phi\]

Electric systems (canonical momentum operator):

\[-i\hbar \nabla \psi=\mathbf{\hat{p}} = m^*\mathbf{\hat{v}}_s + e^* \mathbf{A}\]

We get the London equations again (with an extra factor $\nabla \phi$).

\[\mathbf{J_s} = - \frac{\mathbf{A}}{\mu_0 \lambda_L^2}+\frac{\hbar}{e^{*}}\nabla \phi\]

Asserting single-valuedness of the wavefunction gives flux quantization over a superconducting ring in flux quanta $\Phi_0 = \frac{h}{2e}$

\[\Phi = \frac{\hbar}{2e}\oint_\mathcal{C} \nabla \theta \cdot d\ell =\frac{\hbar}{2e}\cdot 2\pi n \Leftrightarrow \frac{\Phi}{\Phi_0} = n\]

Unconventional superconductors

Conventional have $L=0,S=0$ -> singlet.\

p-wave $L=1$ -> triplet\

d-wave $L=2$ -> Singlet \

In general for $L>0$:

\[\Delta_{\vec{k}} = - \sum_{\vec{k}'} V_{\vec{k}\vec{k}'}\frac{1-2f(E_{\vec{k}'})}{2E_{\vec{k}'}}\]

Generally:

\[\Delta_{\vec{k}} = \Delta_0 \cdot \Phi(\vec{k})\]

Also when $Im(\Phi) \neq 0 \Rightarrow $ Time Symmetry is broken

Breaking time symmetry $\Leftrightarrow$ a Kerr angle effect is measurable (but it is required to account for all Onsager reciprocity effects).

Empiric statements

1) An average of 5/7 free electrons works best for higher Tc (alloys of 6+8 electrons work good). 2) A15 (Beta-Tungsten) structure is necessary for high Tc (metal/conventional) superconductivity 3) There are organic superconductors ($KC_8$ with $T_c = 1\unit{K}$ and $K_3C_{60}$ with $T_c = 18\unit{K}$)
